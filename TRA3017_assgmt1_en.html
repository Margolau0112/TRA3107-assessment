Electricity

Electricity [Note: Electricity is a form of energy that produces heat, light, and power.] is produced in power plants and carried to your home via the electric power grid.

Nonrenewable Energy Resources

Nonrenewable energy sources are those that exist in a limited supply and cannot be replaced or replenished as quickly as they are used. There are two main types of nonrenewable energy sources:

  • 1. Fossil Fuels: Fossil fuels (coal, petroleum/crude oil, and nature gas) formed from the buried remains of plants and animals that lived millions of years ago.
  • 2. Nuclear Energy: Nuclear power is energy in the nucleus (core) of an atom. Splitting atoms produces heat, which makes electricity, in a nuclear power plant.

Renewable Energy Resources


The most common renewable energy sources are
  1. Hydroelectric power, or hydropower, uses flowing water to turn turbines to produce electricity.
  2. Solar energy uses a variety of technologies to convert sunlight to usable energy. Energy from the sun can be used to heat water, heat or cool air, and generate electricity for individual homes or buildings. On a large scale, it can be used to produce electircity to power cities and small towns.
  3. Wind energy uses air blowing across propeller blades casuing them to spin to produce electricity.
